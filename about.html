About My Report

What is AI-Misinformation?

AI-generated misinformation refers to the large-scale production and dissemination of false or misleading content facilitated by artificial intelligence systems. This encompasses deepfakes, hallucinated facts from Large Language Models (LLMs), and automated bot networks designed to polarize public discourse. Unlike traditional misinformation, AI-driven content can be generated at an unprecedented velocity and customized to exploit specific psychological vulnerabilities of target audiences.

Why this topic?

The decision to investigate AI-misinformation stems from the critical junction we currently occupy in the digital age. As generative tools become democratized, the boundary between synthetic and organic truth begins to dissolve. This research seeks to decode the mechanisms of algorithmic deception and its impact on civic trust.

Furthermore, as a student in the MELD 1SS3 program, understanding the linguistic nuances and cultural frameworks that AI utilizes to mimic human communication is essential for developing critical media literacy in an era of post-truth politics.
